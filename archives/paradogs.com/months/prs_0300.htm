PS2 bootlegs

Namco's Ridge Racer V is the first to suffer the fate of the bootlegging business. Apparently the game is on a normal CD in the first place. Ridge Racer 5 was created as Mode2, which is the same exact way a PSX title is done. The main executable of Ridge Racer V is 6 megs in size however, so there is no chance of a Playstation even loading it; the Playstation's two megs of RAM could never handle it.

When the bootleg disc is created, it matches the Ridge Racer V disc exactly. A Playstation 2 unit will not load it though, it just goes back to the menu screen.
