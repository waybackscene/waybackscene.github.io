Pain 10/01 article
PARADOX PSX INTRO COLLECTION - REVIEW
~ review by enzymer

In 1990 there were two cracking groups on the Amiga that had a problem. Trilogy was mainly a strong Danish group with very talented crackers, the other group was M.A.D with mostly French members. Trilogy's problem was that they didn't got enough orginal suppliers (a supplier supplies the groups with new unreleased originals, like games and utilities). M.A.D's problem was that they got the suppliers, but needed skilled crackers. The strongest solution for both teams would be to join forces to have a promising future. In May 1990 these two group decided to join each other and Paradox was formed.

Paradox was strong, too strong in the eyes of many sceners. It was tooo hard to control a group that consist of so many talented members which all got different opinions. In 1991 Paradox died and the best members founded Skid Row.

In 1993 Paradox was rebuilt by some of the old members again. The Amiga pirate scene was dying and Paradox was searching for new grounds and a bigger challenge. An easy target were the consoles, both Genesis and Super Nintendo were hit by the vengeance of Paradox. Especially the Super Nintendo activites were a big race to success. Once again Paradox showed the pirate scene who was the best and was able to give the biggest console groups like Anthrox, Elitendo, Romkids, Legend and Premiere some good challenge.

The PC games got better and better, so the next goal was to conquer the pirate scene on PC and while Paradox was about to succeed, a bust changed the fate of Paradox. In 1994 the leader Maximilien was busted by the police for hacking and that really shocked Paradox, aswell as the whole scene. Conclusion: Paradox died once more. Check the link for an little newspaper article about what he was busted for here: bust

Paradox was put to sleep until 1997 when they suddenly striked back on Playstation with only a few of the old original members. By adding new talents to the main core, Paradox was back on track again fighting for being best in releasing pirated versions of Playstation titles together with another Playstation group called Kalisto. While Kalisto owned the USA releases, Paradox was a very strong force in the hunt for Europe releases. Like it always has been a prediction to what Paradox did, the Gameboy Color and Nintendo 64 were the next systems to conquer soon followed by Dreamcast.

These days, Paradox is moving towards the Playstation 2 and Gameboy Advanced and, god, who knows what the future will bring? One thing that comes to many scener's mind, no matter if they are involved in the pirate scene or the demoscene: Paradox is on one of the most successful groups ever!

Today, Paradox have released both, intros and demos, on various systems too. And while checking their webpage http://www.paradogs.com I found out that they have released a Playstation intro collection that contains the best crackerintros from the most popular groups on Playsation. So I've decided to take a closer look at this collection.
First I've downloaded the intro collection and burned it on a CD. Next thing to do was finding the old dusty Playstation and get it connected to my TV.. done.. it seems to be still working.. inserting the CD... The first thing I saw was a really nice screen with a neat Paradox logo painted by Alien/Paradox and a floating/moving "press start" logo. I pressed the start button and after a screen with credits (as stated on the Paradox webpage the musictrack is not made by NO-XS/Paradox, but actually composed by Jogeir Liljedahl. The programmer Zweifeld/Paradox mixed the tracks up by mistake, so what he thought was the music from NO-XS, was from Jogeir Liljedahl. Never the less, the musictrack is really great.
The next thing that appeared was the menu, were you can choose the intros. It's a simple menu, but easy to control with the Playstation joypad. It reminded me of the old Amiga diskpacks with lots of small productions on it. This collection contains really many intros of various quality, many really good ones. Intros from groups like Accession, Lightforce, TRSI and of course Paradox bring back memories from the good old Amiga days. This collection has so many intros inside that it actually takes some time to see them all. Every intro worked like it should, but one thing that really bugged me is when you've watched a intro you have to reboot the playstation to watch the next one. It would have been perfect if you got the option to go back to the menu again.

If you got a Playstation or if you know someone that does, don't miss this opportunity. Check this tribute to the scene out on a Playstation or even on your own PC, by using an Playstation emulator. It works fine with this emulator: http://adripsx.psxemu.com

And while you're at it, don't forget to download the great Paradox Playstation demos on their webpage http://download.paradogs.com/start.htm

Additional Comments
At first we'd like to thank Enzymer for the fine review and constructive critism about the PlayStation Intro Collection and ofcourse to Unlock for supplying the text source and presenting with Pain a really cool magazine.
A few additional remarks concerning our history mentioned in the review, not to show up or to boost our egos as these are facts which simply can't be denied or forgotten, milestones which simply shouldn't be left out:

PSX:
- the very first group linking intros infront of releases
- the very first group presenting PAL/NTSC selectors
- the very first group presenting trainer versions
- the very first group presenting a real crack (V-Rally 2)
- having introduced PPF to the console scene
- having presented the highest quantity and quality in intros on this platform

PS2:
- having introduced the swap trick to the scene
- the very first group linking intros with PAL/NTSC selector infront of releases

Dreamcast:
- the very first group linking intros infront of releases
- the very first group presenting trainer versions

We use to innovate while others only imitate. ;-)
